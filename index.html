The Journey of Odysseus

Odysseus is a hero of Homer’s epic poem the Odyssey. Odysseus’s homecoming is the theme of the Odyssey. After the Trojan war, most of the Achaean warriors has arrived at their home. However, Odysseus is blown into the realm of marvels, and starts his great wondering. He spent almost nine years to arrives in Ithaca (originally a few weeks journey from Troy to Ithaca).

1. Troy

In the Trojan war, Odysseus appears as the man best suited to cope with crises in personal relations among the Greeks, and he plays a leading part in achieving the reconciliation between Agamemnon and Achilles.

2. Ismarus

In Ismaros, Odysseus and his crew sack a city of the Cicones. However, as a result of underestimating the danger, they lost 72 men. Then, a hurricane drives them way off course, and into the realms of marvels.

3.Land of the Lotus Eaters

On the tenth day, they arrive at the land of the Lotus-Eaters. After eating the lotus, the scouts forget all memory of the journey home.

4. Cyclops

On the cave, they met the Cyclops, Polyphemus. Cyclops are cannibals, living a rude pastoral life. Odysseus escapes death by blinding the Cyclops Polyphemus.

5. The Aeolian Island

Aeolus is the ruler of the winds lives on a floating island. On the island, sons and daughters marry to eac other, and they are always feasting, practicing a divine and non-human society. When Odysseus are going to leave the island, he receives a sack of all the winds except the West Wind as the guest gift.

6. The Land of Laestrygonians

The Lestrygonians have their own civilization (queen and king) and they use tools such as spears. However, Odysseus and his crew face a drastic setback at the hands of the Laestrygonians, a community of violent cannibals.

7. Circe

Circe is the bewitching queen of Aeaea. Circe lures the crew members by her lovely singing, but later drugs them and turns them into pigs. Under the protection of Hermes, Odysseus safe his crews. Circe becomes helpful after Odysseus proves invulnerable to her magic. She tells him what he needs to do to leave.

8. Teiresias and the Land of the Dead

Under Circe’s guidance, Odysseus starts his nekyia, questioning the ghosts and journey to the underworld. the Theban prophet, Tiresias, tells him his fate. He meets a lot of heros in the Trojan war.

9. Circe

Odysseus goes back to Circe’s palace to bury Elpenor. Circe tells him the fate he are going to encounter.

10. Sirens

Sirens are beautiful but dangerous creatures, who lure nearby sailors with their charming song. Sirens know what is the most important thing to their audiences.

11. Charybdis + Scylla

Then, they have tw routes to choose. On the one side is the Wandering Rocks, and on the other side there are Scylla and Charybdis. The prototype of Charybdis may be the whirpool.

12. Thrinacia

On the island of Thrinacia, there pastured the cattle of Helios. Although Odysseus warns that they shoud not kill any cattles on the island, Eurylochus opposes Odysseus’ decision. All of the others crews dies because of the punishment of the shipwreck.

13. Calypso

Calypso is a nymph, who lives on the island of Ogygia. She wants Odysseus to be her husband, so she keeps him on the island for seven years.

14. Phaeacia

Scheria is the land of Phaeacians, the distant relatives of the gods. Alcinous the king, Arete the queen and Nausicaa the princess all treat Odysseus with the best xenia.

15. Ithaca

Finally, Odysseus goes back to his homeland, Ithaca. He is still in constant danger, but it is no longer danger from monsters and goddesses and supernatural forces. Rather, he is now in danger of being killed by Penelope’s suitors.
